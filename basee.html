A1. Base E [Duodozenal]

Tired of using decimal? We, International Base E Society can fix it. The 14dec-base system is relatively satire, but we make it work. The systematic names "Tetradecimal" is a joke, and it uses base decimal anyways. Welcome to our base-neutral base, E.

so how does it work? SImple. we got our digits for decimal, 0123456789, and 4 new alphanumeric digits, abcd. Hex has 6 new digits, and dozenal has 2. dek and el, ↊ or ↋. we could use something called the "argam numerals" for base e, but [flock] you.

the numbers goes like; 0: nil, 1: unt, 2: e, 3: /θeð/, 4: brs, 5: entnil, 6: cwl, 7: flock, 8: unsept, 9: unoctet, a: alpha, b: beta, c: gamma, d: delta. 10 is duodozen, and 20 is biduodozen. for 11-1d, we take out "en" and put the units in the end, so 17 is duodozflock. the multiples of 10 is easy, since you add a specific prefix. 2-9 are the same you use in elements, 2 is bi, 3 is tri, 4 is quad, 5 is pent, 6 is hex, 7 is sept, 8 is oct, 9 is enn. The rest of them are: a is sucenn, b is vet, c is calf and d is dent. so c0 is calfduodozen, 4a is quadduodozalpha and 11 is duodozunt.

NOW, time for the big numbers. 100 is heptominan, and for anything above 100, remove the last letter "n". 101 is heptominaunt. multiples are the same as 10s. so 5dc is pentheptominadentduodozecalf, and 600 is hexheptominan. for 1000, its unt-untfinnio or just untfinnio. 1001 is untfinnio-unt, and 101d is untfinnio-duodozdelta. 2000 is e-untfinnio and 10000 is duodozen-untfinnio. -finnios are just like -illions, so 1000000 [1e6] is efinnio [2-finnio], 1e9 is /θeð/finnio [3-finnio], 1234000 is efinnio-biheptominatriduodozbrs-untfinnio and so on.

Time to go smaller than 1. For fractions; 1/2: half, 1/3: third, 1/4: brsay, ent-nilay, cwlay, 1/7: flockort and the remaining fractions you add -ay. if 2 a's interact they intercept to 1 a. For negatives, its inversiv[num] (or negative [num]), so -6 is inversivcwl. For decimals, decimal point stays the same. (1.248 = unt dot e brs unsept)

A2. Larger numbers [Technicianlogy]

That's for the basics, but some people like to arbituarllu extend numbers to as high as possible. I have thought of this, so I have made a new suffix called "ginnio", which [n]ginnio is the n amount of finnios. (finniostack) /θeð/ginnio is untfinniofinniofinnio, and e-eginnio is efinniofinnio. Same goes with a "-hinnio", which is the amount of ginnios.

Of course, this is infinitely extendible, e.g. the hinnio is the number of ginnios. Googology is the study of larger numbers (more than the average human mindset), and of course we have to do it too. The -herb suffix is the level of -innios. so unherb is finnio, diherb is ginnio, then triherb, quadherb, pentherb etc. You might heard of a "googol", which is 10^100dec, or a googolplex, which is 10^googol[dec]. in base e, a technician is 10^100, and a technicianal is 10^10^100.

A3. Base E in the real world

if we have 7 fingers in each hand, we would be using this base. but even with 5 fingers, counting to duodozen is actually easy. we have exactly duodozen phalanges seperated by grooves in our fingers, so naturally we can count to heptominan using this method. People actually used a similar method to base dozenal.

The symbols [IVXLCDM..] stay the same, but V is now 7 and X is 10. 4 is now IIIV (7-3), 5 is IIV (7-2) and 6 is IV (7-3), which repeats for higher numerals.
